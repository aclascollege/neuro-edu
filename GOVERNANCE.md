# 🏛️ ACLAS Neuro-Edu Governance

This document outlines the decision-making process and project management structure of the ACLAS Neuro-Edu Open Source Initiative.

## ⚖️ Project Philosophy
Neuro-Edu is maintained as a **Research-First** project. Decisions are guided by academic rigor, cognitive science principles, and the goal of educational democratization.

## 👥 Roles

### **Maintainers (The Core Team)**
The **Atlanta College of Liberal Arts and Sciences (ACLAS)** core research team serves as the primary maintainers. They have write access to the main repositories and are responsible for final architectural decisions.

### **Contributors**
Anyone from the community who submits code, documentation, or research findings. After consistent high-quality contributions, a contributor may be invited to join the Maintainer team.

## 🛠️ Decision Making Process

1.  **RFC (Request for Comments)**: Significant changes (API breaks, model architecture shifts) start with an issue labeled `RFC`.
2.  **Consensus**: We aim for consensus among the maintainers.
3.  **Final Say**: In cases where consensus cannot be reached, the ACLAS Lead Researcher holds the final decision-making power to ensure the project remains aligned with its academic mission.

## 🤝 Community Engagement
We hold the community in high regard. Our governance model is designed to be **Transparent but Managed**, ensuring that the project remains stable and safe for educational research use.

---

*This governance model is inspired by successful long-term open source projects like Python and Linux, adapted for the needs of an academic AI framework.*
